Hello Brian!

It is correct that common key signatures with sharps, as a rule, shall have 
their first two accidentals in the same octave, but those with flats shall 
have their first two in different octaves. The first two sharps shall be put 
on the staffs F-line (in treble clef the fifth line representing F5, or 
two-lined f, if you prefer) and in the nearest lower C-space (in the same 
treble clef: C5) respectively. The first two flats shall be put on the 
staffs B-line (in treble clef the third line representing B4) and the 
nearest higher E-space (E5) respectively. According to me these rules should 
be hard and fast, but an old and common practise deviates from them in 
certain circumstances .
