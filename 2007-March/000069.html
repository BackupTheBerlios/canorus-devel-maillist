> It is correct that common key signatures with sharps, as a rule, shall have
> their first two accidentals in the same octave, but those with flats shall
> have their first two in different octaves.
>

I must have been unclear; for a given clef, all the flats for a flat key will
fit within an arbitrary octave (for treble, first space F to fourth space E),
and all sharps will fit within a different octave (for treble, 2nd space A to
G on top of the staff).
I have never seen a key signature in any clef that has its accidentals spread
over more that one octave - if one exists I would like to see it.
