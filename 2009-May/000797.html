Hi guys.

Thanks for the tip. I agree - the theory is one thing and the "useful" 
common practice is usually the other one :)
I remember at this years LAC, we had an interesting talk with Reinhard 
(the Qt4 and pattern maestro) about the Model-Control classes. In 
theory, almost every Model class should have another Control class for 
making the common tasks like insertion, searching, storage etc.

But then in practice, who would use
$ v = Voice()
$ elt = MusElement(v)
$ vCtl = VoiceCtl(v)
$ vCtl.appendAndDoSomething(elt)
$ vCtl.commit()

but rather
$ v = Voice()
$ v.appendAndDoSomething( MusElement(v) )

So the whole theory about the patterns and the idea how to break class 
to pieces probably becomes useful when the particular class gets >1000 
lines long (eg. our MainWindow).

My 5 practical cents. Reinhard, comment? ;)
-Matevž
