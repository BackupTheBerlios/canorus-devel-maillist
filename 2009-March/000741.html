Hi Dag, thanks for the translation.

I'm observing music score editors on regular basis and my personal opinion
is the following:
- NoteEdit: Dead.
- NtEd: One man project. I hope that history won't repeat itself and prof.
Anders opens the project and creates a development team and appropriate site
(versioning, mailing list etc.). Otherwise it ends up as NoteEdit did.
- Denemo: Older project. It's underpowered and is not evolving fast enough
IMO.
- MuseScore: The most promising score editor. It concentrates on the
end-user, score layout and other engraving stuff. I personally don't like
the current GUI and speed. Their philosophy is to create a score editor as
commercial software is (aka Sibelius), but not anything really new yet.
- Canorus: Is evolving more slowely as we planned, but it's getting there.
The philosophy is more innovative, flexible and extendible, lightweight
(similar to firefox maybe). Plugins architecture is something other score
editors don't have to offer yet. We use as much as external projects
(LilyPond, Audiveris, RtMidi, Harmonia) and try to integrate them, so it's
more like a platform maybe.

We had discussions with Werner about the future of our projects (MScore and
Canorus) a few times. While we both produce a score editor, Werner puts most
of his efforts into nice looking scores. Our goal is totally different - we
try to create a good wrapper around Lily to produce nice scores for example.
If we put a lot of engraving options into Canorus, it gets too bloated and
complex and other functionalities like a fast and good composing/arranging
interface suffers. Still, both projects might merge at some point in the
future, but non of them is mature for this yet.

My 5 euro cents :)


Regards.
